Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Networks (DePIN) are blockchain-based systems that use token rewards to incentivize individuals to build, maintain, and operate real-world physical infrastructure. By leveraging a "crowdsourced" approach, DePIN shifts infrastructure development from high-capital, top-down corporate spending to a bottom-up, community-driven model. 
Core Architecture
DePIN projects typically operate through a self-reinforcing "flywheel": 

    Providers: Individuals contribute resources like hardware, bandwidth, or electricity.
    Token Incentives: Providers earn cryptocurrency as compensation for their physical "work" or service provision.
    Decentralized Governance: Often managed by DAOs (Decentralized Autonomous Organizations) that use smart contracts to automate decision-making and reward distribution.
    Physical Integration: Unlike DeFi (which is purely digital), DePIN bridges blockchain incentives with tangible hardware. 

Key Categories & Examples
The sector spans both digital and physical resource networks:
